### Broken

- `/app/dashboard` is a release blocker right now. Opening it directly returns an error page instead of a usable dashboard.
- The `Docs` link is broken everywhere it appears. It goes to `https://docs.regents.sh/`, which returns `404 DEPLOYMENT_NOT_FOUND`.
- The public company page’s `View public output` link is broken on the tested company page. On `/agents/start` it goes to `https://start.regents.sh/`, which also returns `404 DEPLOYMENT_NOT_FOUND`.

### Layout And Arrangement Issues

- The primary `Open app` buttons on the homepage render as blank pills with no visible text. This happens in both light and dark mode.
- The homepage repeats the same three-step story twice in a row: once in the hero rail and again immediately below as three cards. It feels repetitive instead of progressive.
- A rotated “Could not render the Regent scene.” label is visible in the lower-left corner on multiple pages. It looks clipped and accidental rather than intentional.
- The not-found state on `/app/provisioning/test-id` leaves a very large empty area with only one short sentence, so the page feels unfinished and visually broken.
- The direct step pages inside `/app` do not feel consistent with each other. Some show clear blockers and next steps, while others mostly show empty controls and placeholders.

### Copy And Flow Issues

- `/app/dashboard` is the intended post-setup home, but anonymous direct access does not show a blocker or redirect-safe state. It crashes instead.
- `/app/identity` shows the text `alice` inside both claim fields while also showing `Missing name` and leaving the actions disabled. That reads as contradictory and makes the page feel fake or broken.
- `/app/formation` is reachable without signing in and mostly shows empty setup controls. The page does not clearly tell an anonymous visitor that signing in is the blocker.
- The top-right button on the app pages says `Open app` even when the visitor is already inside the app flow. That wording is confusing in context.
- The `Docs` link appears in the main navigation, quickstart area, and footer even though the destination does not exist.
- The public company page promises public output, but the tested public output destination is not live.

### Open Questions

- I did not test real sign-in, wallet connection, pass redemption, name claim, or billing checkout because those would start account or payment flows.
- I did not test a real successful company opening because that requires an account with the right setup state.
- The Autolaunch page produced one aborted navigation during automation before loading normally on retry. I did not see enough evidence to call that a confirmed page bug.
