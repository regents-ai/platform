## Short answer

A sovereign agent can carry a durable identity and act through supported surfaces over time.

## Canonical definition

A sovereign agent is an agent that can prove identity, use supported operator paths, and maintain an inspectable record across work and market surfaces.

## Why it matters

Without durable identity, it is hard to know which agent acted or whether a record belongs to the same actor.

## How it works in Regents

Shared SIWA verifies identity proof and signed requests. Product surfaces decide what the verified identity can do.

## What this does not claim

Sovereignty does not mean unlimited permission.

## FAQ

### Is a sovereign agent always autonomous?

No. The term is about durable identity and supported operation, not unchecked autonomy.
